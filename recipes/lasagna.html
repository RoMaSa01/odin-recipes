Lasagna

Description

Homemade meat lasagna is a traditional and delicious dish that is loved by children and adults alike. It consists of layers of pasta filled with a minced meat bolognese sauce, a creamy béchamel sauce and grated cheese. It is baked until golden brown and au gratin, and served hot or warm. It is an ideal dish to share with family or friends, and can be accompanied by a green salad or garlic bread.

Ingredients

  • 500 g minced meat (a mixture of pork and beef)
  • 2 red peppers
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 150 g bacon or bacon
  • 2 large onions
  • 250 g of natural tomato (about 1 glass)
  • 250 ml of white wine (200 ml. approximately)
  • 100 ml of extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 heaped teaspoon of dried oregano (or Provençal herbs)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper (according to taste)
  • 12 sheets of lasagna or lasagna Garofalo
  • 125 g all-purpose flour
  • 125 g butter
  • 1 liter of whole milk
  • A pinch of nutmeg (about 4 g)
  • 120 g grated Grana Padano cheese, or mix with your favorite gratin cheese

Steps

  1. To make the meat stuffing, put a large pan on the fire with the olive oil and fry the bacon or bacon cut into small pieces until it is golden brown. Add the minced meat and season to taste. Cook over high heat, stirring from time to time, until it loses its pink color and releases its juices.
  2. Meanwhile, chop the peppers, carrots, onions and garlic into small pieces. Add them to the casserole with the meat and lower the heat. Cook for about 15 minutes, stirring from time to time, until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add the natural tomato, the white wine and the dried oregano. Mix well and bring to a boil. Lower the heat and cover the casserole. Cook for an hour and a half, uncovering the casserole for the last 30 minutes so that the sauce reduces. The meat should be tender, but not too shredded, and the sauce should be thick. Taste and adjust salt and pepper if necessary. Reserve the meat filling.
  4. To make the béchamel sauce, put the milk in a saucepan with the bay leaf. Heat it until it starts to boil, turn off the heat and let it infuse. In another saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat and add the flour. Stir with a whisk until you obtain a homogeneous paste without lumps. Cook for a few minutes, stirring constantly, so that the flour is slightly toasted and loses its raw taste.
  5. Remove the bay leaf from the milk and add it little by little to the saucepan with the butter and flour, stirring constantly with the whisk. Cook over medium-low heat for about 10 to 15 minutes, until the béchamel sauce is very thick and smooth. Add salt and pepper to taste and grate a little nutmeg. Add the Parmesan cheese and the egg yolks and mix well. Reserve the béchamel sauce.
  6. Preheat the oven to 180ºC. Grease a large baking dish with a little olive oil. Cook the lasagna sheets in a pot with boiling water and salt for about 20 seconds, just to soften them, and transfer them to a bowl with cold water. Drain the lasagna sheets and dry them with kitchen paper.
  7. Put a layer of pasta in the base of the baking dish. Cover with a layer of meat filling, and then with a layer of bechamel sauce. Repeat the process until all the ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of pasta and bechamel sauce. Sprinkle the grated cheese on top of the lasagna.
  8. Bake the lasagna for about 40 to 45 minutes, or until golden brown and bubbly. Remove from the oven and let it rest for 10 minutes. Cut the lasagna into squares and let it rest for another 10 minutes before serving. This way the layers hold together better and it cools a little faster.
